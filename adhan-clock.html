All Five Prayers
The 24-Hour Clock is Never Empty
Look at the clock. Every hour — from midnight to midnight — is occupied by prayer. There is no gap, no silence, no hour when the Adhan is not sounding somewhere on Earth.

157,890 mosques. Five prayers. 24 hours. The rings fill the entire clock face because the world's Muslims are distributed across every timezone — when it is Fajr in Indonesia, it is Isha in Morocco. The global community of worship never sleeps.
"Indeed, prayer has been decreed upon the believers a decree of specified times." — Quran 4:103
02 / 06
Dhuhr · Solar Noon
The Unbroken Ring
Separate the prayers using the dial. Watch Dhuhr — the golden ring — form the most complete circle of all five prayers. No gaps. No thin patches. A near-perfect ring.

Solar noon moves westward at exactly 15° of longitude per hour — the speed of Earth's rotation. Dhuhr follows it faithfully. At every UTC hour, some longitude is experiencing solar noon. Dhuhr is the only prayer that is permanently, universally active — the anchor of Islamic time.
The gold ring proves what astronomy has always known: the sun never stops moving, and neither does the call to Dhuhr.
03 / 06
Fajr · Dawn
The Circle That Breathes
Separate the prayers and look at the blue Fajr ring. Now change the month slider from June to December. Watch the ring change.

In summer, Fajr disappears at high latitudes — above ~49°N, the sun never dips 18° below the horizon. The ring shows a gap where no Fajr exists astronomically. In winter, the gap closes. The ring breathes with the seasons — expanding and contracting with Earth's axial tilt.

No prayer table, no app has ever made this visible before. Here it is: the seasonal fragility of Fajr, drawn in light.
Switch to June and separate the prayers — watch for the gap in the blue ring above the 18-hour mark.
04 / 06
Maghrib · Sunset
Scholarly Consensus as Geometric Precision
When the prayers are separated, Maghrib's red ring is visibly the sharpest and densest of all five. Its edges are crisp. Its band is thin.

This is not a coincidence. Maghrib is the only prayer on which all four madhabs — Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, Hanbali — agree completely. The trigger is actual sunset: when the upper limb of the sun disappears below the horizon. No interpretation. No angle. Just the sun.

The sharpest ring reflects the least disagreement. Unity produces precision.
The red ring's sharpness is a visual proof of 1,400 years of scholarly consensus.
05 / 06
Isha · Night
One Degree of Difference, Made Visible
Separate the prayers and compare the two outermost rings: Fajr (blue, innermost) and Isha (purple, outermost).

Both are twilight prayers. Both have gaps at high latitudes in summer. But look carefully — Isha's gap is slightly smaller than Fajr's.

The reason: Fajr uses 18° below the horizon. Isha uses 17°. That single degree means Isha remains calculable at slightly higher latitudes before it too disappears. One degree of solar angle, visible as arc length.

This difference — invisible in any prayer time table, invisible in any app — is only visible here.
This is why the clock exists: to reveal what numbers alone cannot show.

The transition between them is the moment of revelation.
