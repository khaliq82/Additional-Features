● Fajr · The Broken Circle
A gap that breathes with the seasons
Fajr is the only prayer with a visible gap. That dark arc = latitudes where the sun never dips 18° below the horizon in summer. Drag to summer — watch the gap open. Drag to winter — watch it close. Earth's axial tilt, made visible.
● Dhuhr · The Unbroken Ring
The only prayer with no gaps, ever
Solar noon happens at every longitude, every day, without exception. No latitude disrupts it. Dhuhr is the anchor of Islamic time — perpetually active somewhere on Earth, forming the most complete ring of all five prayers.
● Asr · The Narrowest Band
The most consistent prayer globally
The shadow ratio definition produces times that vary less across latitudes than twilight prayers. Asr's ring is visibly thinner — the most predictable prayer on Earth. The Prophet ﷺ called it "the middle prayer." Geometrically, it is.
● Maghrib · The Sharpest Edge
Scholarly consensus as geometric precision
All four madhabs agree on Maghrib's trigger: actual sunset. That consensus is visible here — the sharpest, densest ring of all five. The least disagreement produces the most precise band.
● Isha · The Mirror of Fajr
One degree of difference, visible
Isha uses 17° below the horizon vs Fajr's 18°. That single degree means Isha remains calculable at slightly higher latitudes — its gap is measurably smaller than Fajr's. One degree, invisible in any table, visible here as arc length.
● The Separation Dial
Drag to reveal hidden structure
Collapsed: beautiful chaos — all prayers overlapping. Separated: five distinct orbits, each telling its own story. The moment of separation is when the structure of Islamic time becomes legible.
