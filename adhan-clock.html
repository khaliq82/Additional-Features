● Fajr · The Broken Circle
A gap that breathes with the seasons
Fajr is the only prayer with a visible gap — latitudes where the sun never dips 18° below the horizon in summer. Change month to June and watch the gap open. In December it closes. Earth's axial tilt, made visible.
● Dhuhr · The Unbroken Ring
The only prayer with no gaps, ever
Solar noon happens at every longitude, every day, without exception. No latitude, no season disrupts it. Dhuhr is the anchor of Islamic time — the most complete ring across all twelve months.
● Asr · The Narrowest Band
The most consistent prayer globally
The shadow ratio produces times that vary less across latitudes than twilight prayers. Asr's ring is visibly thinner — the most predictable prayer on Earth. The Prophet ﷺ called it "the middle prayer." Geometrically, it is.
● Maghrib · The Sharpest Edge
Scholarly consensus as precision
All four madhabs agree on Maghrib: actual sunset. That consensus is visible here — the sharpest, densest ring. The least disagreement produces the most precise band.
● Isha · The Mirror of Fajr
One degree of difference, visible
Isha uses 17° below the horizon vs Fajr's 18°. That single degree means Isha's gap is measurably smaller. One degree, invisible in any table, visible here as arc length.
● The Separation Dial
Drag to reveal hidden structure
Collapsed: all prayers as one ring — the beautiful chaos of global worship. Separated: five distinct orbits, each telling its own story. The structure of Islamic time becomes legible.
